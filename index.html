Non-Medical Determinants of Health

An Interactive Data Visualization Project Using OECD Health Statistics

Background

This interactive data visualization project explores the non-medical factors that significantly influence public health across OECD countries. Using comprehensive data from 2010 onwards, we examine various lifestyle factors and their potential impact on population health outcomes.

The visualization focuses on four key areas that are crucial determinants of public health:

Tobacco Use

Tracking daily smoking rates and vaping product usage across different age groups and genders.

Alcohol Consumption

Measuring annual alcohol consumption patterns in liters per person.

Nutrition Habits

Analyzing daily fruit and vegetable consumption, along with fat, calorie, protein, and sugar supply.

Weight Status

Examining overweight and obesity rates across different demographics.

Project Objectives

  • Provide interactive visualizations that allow users to explore relationships between different health determinants across OECD countries
  • Enable comparison of health indicators across different demographic groups (age, gender) and time periods
  • Identify trends and patterns in lifestyle factors that may influence public health outcomes
  • Create an accessible and intuitive interface for both general audiences and health professionals
  • Support evidence-based decision making by presenting complex health data in an understandable format

Data & Methodology

Our visualizations are built using D3.js, incorporating data from the OECD's comprehensive health statistics database. The data covers multiple dimensions including:

  • Time period: From 2010 onwards
  • Demographics: Age groups (15+ years, 15-24 years) and gender
  • Measurement methods: Including surveys, self-reporting, and direct measurements
  • Various units of measure: Percentages, grams per person, liters per person, etc.
